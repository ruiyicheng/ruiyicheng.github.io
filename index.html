Research Projects

Tianyu Project
Jun 2023 – Present
Full-stack developer for observation control, data processing, and scheduling systems of a 1-m time-domain survey telescope.
  • Work on a 1 m optical survey telescope in Lenghu, Qinghai, producing light curves from sub-second to year-long timescales over a 10–20 mag range (≈ 10,000× in flux), generating ~20 TB of light curves and ~40 TB of images per year for exoplanet searches, transient detection, and time-series analysis.
  • Proposed scientific goals, designed survey mode, and estimated scientific yield.
  • Designed a scalable distributed data-processing pipeline for light-curve extraction using a MySQL + RabbitMQ–based distributed DAG system; achieved near-theoretical noise limits and minute-cadence observations on real data.
  • Proposed DeepAP: a two-stage deep learning framework for aperture photometry using a ViT for feasibility classification and a ResNet for aperture-size selection, achieving ~59,000× speed-up over classical methods and enabling large-scale sub-second cadence light-curve extraction.
  • Developed LenghuSky-8, an 8-year, 429k-image all-sky camera dataset from Lenghu, Qinghai (CVPR 2026 submitted); performed absolute astrometric calibration, annotated cloud masks, and benchmarked cloud segmentation and weather nowcasting models (DINOv3 linear probe & Conv-LSTM perform best).
  • Developing a real-time camera control system for online image stacking and astrometric solving feeding DeepAP and a minute-cadence light-curve pipeline.
  • Designing a hierarchical MILP scheduler for multi-telescope, multi-filter surveys and follow-up observations, with an LLM-based target-selection tool mining arXiv papers.
  • Implementing GPU-accelerated variability analysis (Lomb–Scargle periodograms, two-point correlations, von Neumann indices) for large-scale light-curve datasets.
Mitigating Astrometric Bias in Barycentric Correction
Dec 2021 – Jun 2023
  • Developed a gradient-based method to correct biases in stellar astrometric parameters, enabling high-precision barycentric correction of radial-velocity time series for Earth-twin exoplanet searches.
  • Demonstrated that proper treatment of astrometric systematics is critical for ultra-precise time-series analysis in radial-velocity data.
Thermodynamic Properties of 2D JT Gravity and the SYK Model
Feb 2020 – Jun 2021
  • Numerically computed complex-time Green functions and studied thermodynamic properties of 2D Jackiw–Teitelboim (JT) gravity and the Sachdev–Ye–Kitaev (SYK) model.
  • Estimated critical exponents and gained extensive experience in numerical analysis, scientific computing, and large-scale simulations.
